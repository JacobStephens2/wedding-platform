### Why 29 is risky, even with cash funds:
1.  **The "Grandma Factor":** Roughly 20–30% of guests (especially older generations) fundamentally refuse to give cash. They view it as impersonal or "paying an entrance fee." If they see an empty registry, they will go rogue and buy you random crystal bowls or art you don't want. [theknot](https://www.theknot.com/content/how-many-gifts-to-register-for)
2.  **Visual Psychology:** When a registry looks "picked over" (i.e., only 3 expensive items left), guests feel late and guilty. They panic and buy a random generic gift card or a physical item from a store not on your list.
3.  **Shower Gifts:** Guests *never* give cash at bridal showers. You need physical boxes for them to wrap and you to open. 29 items will be wiped out by the shower alone, leaving nothing for the wedding itself. [washingtonian](https://washingtonian.com/2015/04/15/must-have-registry-items-and-other-pro-tips-from-macys-registry-team/)

### The Modified "Cash-Heavy" Strategy

*   **Role of Items:** These exist solely to satisfy traditional guests and shower attendees.
*   **Role of Funds:** These are your primary goal.

### Price Band Guidance (for ~150 guests)
If you're aiming for **50–75 physical registry items**, the goal is to have *many* low/mid-priced options and only a handful of high-ticket items.

#### Price bands (used for admin reporting)
- **Under $25**: \( $0.01 \le price < $25 \)
- **$25–$50**: \( $25 \le price < $50 \)
- **$50–$100**: \( $50 \le price < $100 \)
- **$100–$200**: \( $100 \le price < $200 \)
- **$200–$400**: \( $200 \le price < $400 \)
- **$400+**: \( $400 \le price \)
- **No price**: missing/blank/0 (try to avoid this; it makes planning harder)

#### Suggested mix (percent of total items)
- **Under $25**: 10–15%
- **$25–$50**: 25–35%
- **$50–$100**: 25–30%
- **$100–$200**: 15–20%
- **$200–$400**: 5–10%
- **$400+**: 0–5%
